Pi Neuron Simulator

Pi is a biological neuron simulator. The simulation is based on the belief that neurons are built of sets of functionally different sub-components. The sub-components can change their own operating state, and in turn affect the state and functionality of other comparments in the neuron.

The goal of Pi is to be able to build simulations of cortical columns and micro-circuits, focusing mostly on collective behavior of the neurons.

The sandbox is available here. The site may or may not be functional.
The project page is available on GitHub.
WhiteRabbit3DHTML + CSS + 3D

The WhiteRabbit3D is a technology that allows standard 2 dimensional content to be easily laid out and positioned in 3D space. The WhiteRabbit browser is an implementation of this for HTML, where the 3D aspects are expressed as CSS styles, hence, CSS-3D. More content can be displayed on a page and the placement makes the information more readily understandable.

Bio

I'm a Canadian engineer with a graduate degree in Vehicle Dynamics and Control Systems. After racing Karts in Canada and abroad I moved to England where I was manager and lead dynamicist of the Vehicle Dynamics department at Reynard Motorsport. I working on their Indy Car, Le Mans, Formula Nippon, and American Le Mans projects as well as being contracted to British American Formula 1 Team.

After leaving racing I moved to Silicon Valley, working for SideStep, Athoc, Amazon's A9 and YouSendIt, later renamed Hightail.

I've maintained an interest in 3D user interfaces, outrigger canoe boath and paddle design, and from my Master's a longer term investigation into neurosciences, mainly cortical dynamics and learning. A recent diversion has been an exploration of molecular level inductive interactions among bond electrons.
